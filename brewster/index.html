Brewster's Millions is the story of how one man, desiring the American Dream, got more than he bargained for.
Monty Brewster was a Minor League baseball player on a road to nowhere. However, a stroke of luck ensured he was the last living heir of one of the richest men in America, a sadistic bastard who set Brewster a challenge in his will:
To spend thirty million dollars in a single month while accumulating no assets.
If he succeeded he would be rewarded with the full inheritance of $300 million
If he failed, he would get nothing.

The challenge had strict rules:

  1. He could not accumulate any assets, such as houses, cars, stocks and bonds.
  2. He had to return in exactly thirty days with nothing but the shirt on his back.
  3. He needed receipts for all purchases(handled by an accountant supplied by the executor of the estate).
  4. He could hire anyone he wanted, but had to get value for their services.
  5. He could not destroy anything inherently valuable (no buying and burning Picassos...)
  6. He could not simply buy a large gift and give it away, although he could do so for smaller gifts (this is not very well explained in the movie).
  7. He could only gamble away 5%.
  8. He could only give 5% to charity.
  9. He wasn't allowed to tell anyone why he needed to spend the money.

Sounds difficult, right? Well, it made a fun movie!
